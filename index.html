What is a Baithak?

A baithak is not a concert. It's a gathering ‚Äî traditionally held in homes, where musicians and listeners sit together in close proximity. There's no stage, no spotlight, no applause at predetermined moments. Just music, shared in its most human form.

Living Room Baithaks revives this tradition in Bangalore, creating intimate spaces for raagdhari music ‚Äî classical, semi-classical, and exploratory forms rooted in the raga tradition.

Why Are We Doing This?

To bring listening back. In a world of distracted consumption, we create a space where music is the only thing happening. No phones, no interruptions ‚Äî just presence.

To sustain artists. Every contribution goes directly to the musicians. This is how baithaks have always worked ‚Äî listeners supporting artists, ensuring the music continues.

To build community. Not an audience, but a community ‚Äî people who return, who listen deeply, who ask questions, who bring friends. This is how traditions stay alive.

Curation

Each baithak is thoughtfully curated by Mowna Ramachandra, alongside the performing artists. Every concert is shaped with intention:

  • Traditional repertoire: Time-tested bandishes and compositions
  • Contemporary explorations: New interpretations and experiments within the raga framework
  • Guided listening: Conversations and explanations woven into the music, making it accessible to first-time listeners and enriching for seasoned rasikas

We honor the guru-shishya tradition while welcoming diverse musical voices ‚Äî Hindustani, Carnatic, and beyond.
